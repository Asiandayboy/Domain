1. Edge AI (Artificial Intelligence)

Edge AI enables embedded devices to process AI models locally using frameworks like TensorFlow Lite and PyTorch Mobile. Applications include real-time object detection, voice recognition, and predictive maintenance â€” all without relying on cloud infrastructure. Popular hardware: NVIDIA Jetson, Google Coral, and ARM Cortex-M with DSP extensions.

Source: NVIDIA Embedded Developer Portal, TensorFlow Lite

2. Real-Time Operating Systems (RTOS)

RTOSes like FreeRTOS, Zephyr, and VxWorks are designed for deterministic performance. They manage tasks, timing, and hardware events predictably, which is essential for applications like medical devices, robotics, and aerospace systems.

Source: FreeRTOS.org, Zephyr Project, VxWorks by Wind River

3. Embedded Linux

Embedded Linux is a powerful platform for feature-rich embedded applications, offering multitasking, networking, and filesystem support. Systems like Yocto, Buildroot, and OpenWRT help developers customize Linux for constrained hardware like Raspberry Pi, BeagleBone, and custom ARM boards.

Source: Yocto Project, Buildroot, OpenWRT

4. Secure IoT and Embedded Security

As devices become network-connected, embedded systems must be hardened with security technologies: Secure Boot, TLS, Hardware Root of Trust (TPM), and OTA firmware signing are becoming standard. Tools like Mbed TLS and wolfSSL are widely used to encrypt and protect data on the edge.

Source: Mbed TLS, wolfSSL, NXP Secure Boot

5. Low-Power Design and Energy Harvesting

Battery-powered and remote devices depend on ultra-low-power microcontrollers and sleep-state management. Technologies like ARM's Cortex-M0+, EFM32, and energy harvesting sensors enable long-term deployments with minimal energy budgets.

Source: Silicon Labs EFM32, ARM Cortex-M0+

6. Wireless Communication Protocols

Embedded systems use protocols like Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE), Zigbee, LoRaWAN, and NB-IoT for efficient, low-power communication. These technologies enable smart agriculture, remote sensors, and wearable health devices to operate over long distances with low energy use.

Source: Bluetooth LE, Zigbee Alliance, LoRa Alliance, GSMA on NB-IoT
